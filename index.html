US 10Y - 2Y Spread

Yield curve spread history

What this signal means

The spread between longer-term and shorter-term government bond yields helps show whether the yield curve is normal or inverted.

Interpretation

A negative spread means the curve is inverted. This has historically been monitored as a possible recession warning sign.

Methodology

  • Spread = 10-year Treasury yield minus shorter maturity yield.
  • 10Y-2Y uses the US 10-year yield minus the US 2-year yield.
  • 10Y-3M uses the US 10-year yield minus the US 3-month yield.
  • Curve status is labeled as inverted when the spread is below 0.00 percentage points.
  • Days inverted counts the current consecutive period below zero.
